Documentation

ModCRE DB

ModCRE DB annotates human transcription-factor sequence records with DNA-binding motif information from public PWM databases, nearest-neighbour sequence transfer and structure-based modelling with ModCRE.

The database is intended for motif lookup, provenance-aware PWM selection, structural inspection of model-supported entries, and sequence scanning against generated PWM matrices.

Scope

The input set was compiled from human UniProt records associated with transcription and DNA binding, followed by negative filtering for DNA repair, recombination, replication, modification, nucleosome-related and other non-TF DNA-associated functions.

Entries are treated at the UniProt sequence-record level. This preserves isoforms and TrEMBL records that would otherwise be collapsed in a gene-level catalogue.

Use cases

  • Retrieve motif annotations for a TF accession, gene name, protein name, PFAM family or motif identifier.
  • Compare direct, transferred and structure-derived motif assignments before choosing a PWM for analysis.
  • Download MEME-format motif files and inspect generated sequence logos.
  • Scan user-provided DNA against selected motifs or the global generated-PWM collection.
  • Open model-supported TF-DNA structures when 3D models are indexed for the entry.

Construction workflow

ordered by annotation strength

Direct PWM matching

JASPAR, CisBP and HOCOMOCO are queried for motif records matching the TF accession or database-specific TF identifiers.

Nearest-neighbour transfer

Unassigned sequences are compared against PWM-bearing TFs with MMseqs2 and separated into >70% and 50–70% identity tiers.

ModCRE

Remaining modellable TFs are submitted to ModCRE to derive PWMs from modelled protein-DNA interfaces.

AlphaFold3 + ModCRE

For unresolved cases, AlphaFold3-derived TF-DNA structures are converted and processed with ModCRE when the interface passes structural checks.

Motif annotation categories

shown from strongest to most inferred assignment

The primary category is the strongest available assignment for the TF record. Additional motif links may still be shown on TF and motif pages.

CategoryAssignment ruleHow to read it
Known Direct PWM match in JASPAR, CisBP or HOCOMOCO. Use as the strongest motif assignment for the corresponding TF record.
Nearest Neighbor (>70%) PWM transferred from a closely related TF with at least 70% sequence identity in the operational MMseqs2 search. High-confidence transferred motif; source TF and identity should remain visible.
Nearest Neighbor (50–70%) PWM transferred from a more distant annotated TF in the 50–70% identity interval. Candidate motif assignment; appropriate for exploratory analyses or weighted scans.
ModCRE Structure-based PWM derived by ModCRE from a modellable protein-DNA interface. Useful when direct and close homology information is absent; inspect model/template details.
AlphaFold3-assisted ModCRE AlphaFold3 TF-DNA structure processed through ModCRE after interface checks. Most inferred motif layer; structure and interface quality should be considered before downstream use.

Generated PWMs and DNA scanning

A motif file is labelled Generated PWM when a usable A/C/G/T probability matrix is available for sequence-logo rendering and DNA scanning. Entries marked Missing MEME are retained as provenance records but are not scanned.

The scanner accepts pasted DNA or FASTA sequence and reports ranked motif occurrences with motif identifier, source, TF link, annotation category, strand, genomic interval within the submitted sequence, score and p-value.

Structure-derived records

ModCRE models TF-DNA recognition from structural information and derives PWMs from the protein-DNA interface. In ModCRE DB, ModCRE records are kept separate from direct and nearest-neighbour assignments.

Where available, 3D model pages expose the indexed PDB model, source, template/model identifier, residue range and linked motif records. AlphaFold3-assisted entries use predicted TF-DNA structures as input to the same ModCRE PWM derivation step.
